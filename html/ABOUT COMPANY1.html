ABOUT THE FOUNDER OF HERBLIFE




Mark Reynolds Hughes

AN INSPIRING PERSONALITY

Mark Reynolds Hughes was an American businessman who was the founder, chairman, and CEO of Herbalife International Ltd, a multi-level marketing company.He was born in La Mirada, California in 1956, the son of Jack Reynolds and Jo Ann Hughes. His parents divorced in 1970, when Mark was only 14 years old, and later his mother took care of him all alone, the family survived on welfare payments with Hughes' mother suffering from "emotional problems." She battled obesity and tension with the help of supplements like amphetamines and sleeping pills. In the ninth standard Hughes dropped out of school and started taking drugs.

Once he stated "I was a little delinquent," he further stated "I got in trouble with the law." At the age of 16, he was sent to CEDU (community employment development unit) High School, a private residential school for troubled youngsters in Running Springs, Part of Hughes' CEDU rehab required him to raise money by selling raffle tickets. Hughes became the school's best salesman. "The approval there was based on how much funds you raised," Hughes told in People magazine in 1985 that "There was a lot of pressure, and I wanted to be the best right away."

Hughes was 19 years old and still working for CEDU when his mother died in her apartment due to accidental drug overdose on April 27, 1975. According to the post-mortem examination, several empty bottles of her prescription drugs were found just beside her bed, and her doctor told that “she was known to over-ingest her prescription drugs “. In 1976, the 20-year-old Hughes began selling diet products for Seyforth Laboratories, a multilevel marketer, and very soon he became one of its top 100 earners. After Seyforth collapsed in 1979, Hughes then sold exercise equipment and weight-control products for a company called Golden Youth. While working for Golden Youth in 1979 that Hughes met Kathryn Whiting, who was a former "Miss Santa Monica", and a 21-year old college student pursuing a degree in medical science. She became the first wife of Hughes.

According to a report Hughes married 4 times. When Golden Youth also went out of business, Hughes decided to start his own operation. In February 1980, when Hughes was 24years old, He founded Los Angeles-based Herbalife International. It has since become one of the world's largest distributors of Herbalife products through multi-level marketing, with sales of about $3.5 billion in 2007 and 2.1 million Independent Distributors. Now in 95 countries and achieving record retail sales of $7.5 billion in 2013 according to company statements.

ABOUT COMPANY

Herbal life Nutrition products include weight-loss and protein shakes, as well as protein bars, teas, aloes, vitamins, and sports hydration, energy, and personal care products, which they sell by the names such as WEIGHT MANAGEMENT PRODUCTS, ENERGY PRODUCTS, SPORTS NUTRITIONS, SKIN HEALTH, CHILDREN HEALTH PRODUCTS, DIGESTIVE HEALTH PROCTS, BONE AND JOINT HEALTH PROCTS, CARDIOVASCULAR HEALTH PRODUCTS, ENHANCERS ETC., The company's first product is the Formula 1 protein shake which is the very first owned product of the company, a soya- shake. The product was out in 1980 and, as of 2015, was the company's best selling product accounting for nearly 30% of total sales.Herbal life products are been processed at the company's five manufacturing facilities in the U.S. and China as well as third-party manufacturing partners and Herbalife India currently manufactures its complete range of inner nutrition products through third-party contract manufacturers at Baddi and Paonta Sahib in Himachal Pradesh.

The company's production process is based on a 'seed to feed' strategy which the company initiated in the 2010s which means grow your own plants and vegetation to feed that is the company started to grow its own herbs for extracts and allows it trace where the ingredients in its nutritional products originated. Since 2013, the company has operated a botanical extraction facility in Changsha, Hunan Province. The facility produces botanical extracts, including teas, guarana, chamomile, broccoli, and bilberry, for use in many of the company's products. Before extracts are processed they undergo a botanical identification program and are tested several times during the production process. The processed raw materials from the extraction facility are used at all of the company's branded manufacturing facilities as well as its partners. As of 2015, 58% of the company's nutrition products were manufactured from Herbal life owned facilities.

In China, the company's manufacturing sites are located in Suzhou, Nanjing. In the U.S., the company has manufacturing facilities in Lake Forest, California and Winston-Salem, North Carolina.
